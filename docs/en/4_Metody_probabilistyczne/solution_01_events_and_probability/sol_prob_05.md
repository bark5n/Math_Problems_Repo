# Task 5 – Probability of Water Volume Intervals

**Problem:**

Let:

A = {water volume ∈ ⟨125, 250⟩}  
B = {water volume ∈ (200, 300⟩}

Given:

P(A) = 0.6  
P(B) = 0.7  
P(A ∪ B) = 0.8  

Calculate:

1) P(A′)  
2) P(A ∩ B)  
3) P(A′ ∩ B′)  

---

## Solution

### 1) Complement of A

P(A′) = 1 − P(A)

P(A′) = 1 − 0.6  
P(A′) = **0.4**

---

### 2) Intersection of A and B

Using the formula:

P(A ∩ B) = P(A) + P(B) − P(A ∪ B)

P(A ∩ B) = 0.6 + 0.7 − 0.8  
P(A ∩ B) = 1.3 − 0.8  
P(A ∩ B) = **0.5**

---

### 3) Probability that volume falls into neither interval

A′ ∩ B′ = (A ∪ B)′

Using complement rule:

P(A′ ∩ B′) = 1 − P(A ∪ B)

P(A′ ∩ B′) = 1 − 0.8  
P(A′ ∩ B′) = **0.2**
