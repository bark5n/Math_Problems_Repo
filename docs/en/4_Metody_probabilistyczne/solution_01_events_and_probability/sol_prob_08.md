### Task 8 Answer

**Problem:**

Coded information consists of 7 pulses:  

- 4 pulses of type \(A\)  
- 2 pulses of type \(B\)  
- 1 pulse of type \(C\)  

Assuming a random arrangement of pulses, find the probability that:

1. The first received pulse will be \(A\)  
2. The first received pulse will be \(A\) or \(C\)  
3. The first two pulses will be \(A\) and \(C\) in that order

---

**Solution:**

Total number of pulses: \(7 = 4+2+1\)

1. **Probability that the first pulse is \(A\)**  

\[
P(\text{first pulse = A}) = \frac{\text{number of A pulses}}{\text{total pulses}} = \frac{4}{7}
\]

2. **Probability that the first pulse is \(A\) or \(C\)**  

\[
P(\text{first pulse = A or C}) = \frac{4+1}{7} = \frac{5}{7}
\]

3. **Probability that the first two pulses are \(A\) and \(C\) in that order**  

- First pulse \(A\): \(4/7\)  
- Second pulse \(C\): only 1 \(C\) remains, total remaining pulses = 6 → \(1/6\)  

\[
P(\text{first = A, second = C}) = \frac{4}{7} \cdot \frac{1}{6} = \frac{4}{42} = \frac{2}{21}
\]

---

**Answer:**

\[
P(\text{first pulse = A}) = \frac{4}{7}, \quad
P(\text{first pulse = A or C}) = \frac{5}{7}, \quad
P(\text{first two pulses = A then C}) = \frac{2}{21}
\]
