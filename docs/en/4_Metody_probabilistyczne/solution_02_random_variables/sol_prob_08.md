## Task 8 – Joint Distribution of Independent Random Variables

**Problem:**

Two people from city A are trying to establish a telephone connection with city B.

Random variables:

X – number of attempts by person 1  
Y – number of attempts by person 2

Distributions:

For X:

P(X=1) = 0.6  
P(X=2) = 0.4

For Y:

P(Y=1) = 0.5  
P(Y=2) = 0.5

Attempts are independent.

---

### Step 1 – Use independence

For independent random variables:

P(X=x , Y=y) = P(X=x) · P(Y=y)

---

### Step 2 – Compute probabilities

P(X=1 , Y=1)

= 0.6 × 0.5  
= **0.3**

P(X=1 , Y=2)

= 0.6 × 0.5  
= **0.3**

P(X=2 , Y=1)

= 0.4 × 0.5  
= **0.2**

P(X=2 , Y=2)

= 0.4 × 0.5  
= **0.2**

---

### Step 3 – Joint distribution table

| X \ Y | 1 | 2 |
|------|----|----|
| 1 | 0.3 | 0.3 |
| 2 | 0.2 | 0.2 |

Check:

0.3 + 0.3 + 0.2 + 0.2 = 1 ✔
