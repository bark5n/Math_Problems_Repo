# Task 3 – Probability of Time and Errors

**Problem:**

Person X performs a job in 4, 5, or 6 hours and may commit 0, 1, or 2 errors.

All 9 possible elementary events (time, number of errors) are equally likely.

Total number of elementary events:
3 (time options) × 3 (error options) = 9

Each elementary event has probability:

P = 1/9

---

## 1) The job will be completed in 4 hours

Possible outcomes:
(4,0), (4,1), (4,2)

Number of favorable outcomes = 3

P(4 hours) = 3/9 = **1/3**

---

## 2) The job will be completed flawlessly in 6 hours

Flawless means 0 errors.

Possible outcome:
(6,0)

Number of favorable outcomes = 1

P(6 hours and 0 errors) = 1/9

---

## 3) The job will be completed in at most 5 hours

At most 5 hours → 4 or 5 hours

Possible outcomes:
(4,0), (4,1), (4,2),
(5,0), (5,1), (5,2)

Number of favorable outcomes = 6

P(time ≤ 5) = 6/9 = **2/3**

---

## 4) The job will be completed in at most 5 hours and with at most one error

Time ≤ 5 → 4 or 5  
Errors ≤ 1 → 0 or 1

Possible outcomes:
(4,0), (4,1),
(5,0), (5,1)

Number of favorable outcomes = 4

P(time ≤ 5 and errors ≤ 1) = 4/9
