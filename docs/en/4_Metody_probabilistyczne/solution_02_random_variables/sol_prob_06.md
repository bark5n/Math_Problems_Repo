## Task 6 – Joint Distribution of Two Random Variables

**Problem:**

A mechanism consists of two gears: a **large gear** and a **small gear**.

Define two zero–one random variables:

X = 1 if the large gear has a **positive deviation ("plus")**  
X = 0 if the large gear has a **negative deviation ("minus")**

Y = 1 if the small gear has a **positive deviation ("plus")**  
Y = 0 if the small gear has a **negative deviation ("minus")**

Given probabilities:

P(X=0, Y=0) = 1/4  
P(X=1, Y=1) = 1/4  

(these correspond to **bad assembly**, because both gears have the same deviation)

P(X=0, Y=1) = 1/4  
P(X=1, Y=0) = 1/4  

(these correspond to **correct assembly**, because the deviations are different)

---

### Step 1 – Joint probability distribution table

| X \ Y | 0 | 1 |
|------|----|----|
| 0 | 1/4 | 1/4 |
| 1 | 1/4 | 1/4 |

This table represents the **joint probability distribution** of the random variables **X** and **Y**.

---

### Step 2 – Probability that the assembly is correct

Correct assembly occurs when the deviations are **different**:

(X = 0, Y = 1)  
(X = 1, Y = 0)

Therefore:

P(correct assembly)  
= P(X=0, Y=1) + P(X=1, Y=0)

P(correct assembly)  
= 1/4 + 1/4

P(correct assembly) = **1/2**

---

### Final Result

Joint distribution:

| X \ Y | 0 | 1 |
|------|----|----|
| 0 | 1/4 | 1/4 |
| 1 | 1/4 | 1/4 |

Probability that the assembly is correct:

**P = 1/2**
