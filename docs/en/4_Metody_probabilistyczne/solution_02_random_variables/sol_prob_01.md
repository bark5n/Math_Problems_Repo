```markdown id="6gq3b1"
## Task 1

Let **X** denote the grade of a randomly selected student from a group of 10 students.  
Since the experiment is **randomly selecting one student**, and the outcome (the grade) is a **numerical value**, **X is a random variable**.

The possible values of the random variable are:

X ∈ {2, 3, 4, 5}

Given grades:
5, 4, 3, 3, 4, 5, 3, 3, 4, 2

We determine probabilities using relative frequency:

P(X = x) = (number of occurrences of grade x) / 10  
"P of X equals x equals number of occurrences of grade x divided by ten"

Counting each grade:

Grade 2 → 1 occurrence  
P(X = 2) = 1/10  
"P of X equals two equals one over ten"

Grade 3 → 4 occurrences  
P(X = 3) = 4/10  
"P of X equals three equals four over ten"

Grade 4 → 3 occurrences  
P(X = 4) = 3/10  
"P of X equals four equals three over ten"

Grade 5 → 2 occurrences  
P(X = 5) = 2/10  
"P of X equals five equals two over ten"

Thus, **X is a discrete random variable**, and its probability distribution is:

| x      | 2   | 3   | 4   | 5   |
|------  |---- |---- |---- |---- |
| P(X=x) |1/10 |4/10 |3/10 |2/10 |
```
